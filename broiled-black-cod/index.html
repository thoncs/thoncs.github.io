Broiled Black Cod

Sablefish, also known as black cod, is a delicacy known for its rich, buttery flavor and delicate texture. Cooking it under a broiler after marinating in a blend of white miso paste, mirin, sugar, soy sauce, and sake can elevate this dish to a gourmet level. Here's a step-by-step guide to achieving a perfect broiled sablefish.


### Ingredients

- **Sablefish fillets** (about 6 ounces each)
- **White miso paste** (2 tablespoons)
- **Mirin** (2 tablespoons)
- **Sugar** (1 tablespoon)
- **Soy sauce** (2 tablespoons)
- **Sake** (2 tablespoons)
- **Sesame oil** (1 teaspoon)
- **Garlic** (2 cloves, minced)
- **Ginger** (1 teaspoon, grated)
- **Green onions** (for garnish)
- **Lemon wedges** (for serving)

### Instructions

#### Step 1: Prepare the Marinade

1. **Combine the marinade ingredients** in a bowl. Mix together the white miso paste, mirin, sugar, soy sauce, sake, sesame oil, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Stir until the sugar is dissolved and the mixture is well combined. 

2. **Marinate the sablefish** fillets. Place the fillets in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them. Ensure the fillets are evenly coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 hours for deeper flavor. 

#### Step 2: Preheat the Broiler

1. **Preheat your broiler** to high. Position the oven rack about 6 inches from the heat source. 

#### Step 3: Broil the Sablefish

1. **Remove the fillets from the marinade** and pat them dry with paper towels. This step helps achieve a crispy skin. 

2. **Place the fillets on a broiler pan** or a baking sheet lined with aluminum foil. Brush the fillets with a bit of the leftover marinade to enhance flavor and prevent drying. 

3. **Broil the fillets** for about 4-5 minutes on each side, or until the skin is crispy and the fish is cooked through. The internal temperature should reach 145°F (63°C). 

#### Step 4: Serve

1. **Garnish the fillets** with chopped green onions and serve with lemon wedges on the side. The acidity of the lemon complements the rich flavor of the sablefish. 

### Tips for Success

- **Broiler Pan**: Using a broiler pan allows excess fat to drip away from the fish, preventing flare-ups and ensuring even cooking.
- **Watch Closely**: Keep a close eye on the fillets while broiling to avoid overcooking, which can make the fish dry and tough.
- **Flavor Variations**: Feel free to experiment with the marinade ingredients. Adding a splash of rice vinegar or a pinch of red pepper flakes can add an extra layer of flavor.

### Conclusion

Broiling sablefish after marinating it in a mixture of white miso paste, mirin, sugar, soy sauce, and sake results in a dish that is both flavorful and elegant. This method brings out the natural richness of the fish while adding a delightful umami depth from the marinade. Enjoy this gourmet experience at home with a simple yet effective technique.
